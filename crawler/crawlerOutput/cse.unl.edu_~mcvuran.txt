<h2><font color="#006699">BIOGRAPHY</font></h2>
<p>Mehmet Can Vuran was born in Istanbul, Turkey. He received his B.Sc. degree in Electrical and Electronics Engineering from <a href="http://www.bilkent.edu.tr" target="no">Bilkent University</a> ,
Ankara, Turkey in 2002. He received his M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the Broadband and Wireless Networking Laboratory,
School of Electrical and Computer Engineering, <a href="http://www.gatech.edu" target="no">Georgia Institute of Technology</a>, Atlanta, GA. in 2004 and
2007, respectively, under the guidance of Prof. Ian F. Akyildiz.</p>
<p>Currently, he is an Associate Professor in the Department of Computer
  Science and Engineering at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. Dr. Vuran was
  recognized as a <b><a href="http://highlycited.com" target="no">Highly Cited Researcher</a></b> in 2014 by <b>Thomson Reuters</b> <i>"in
  recognition of ranking among the top 1\% of researchers for most cited
  documents in Computer Science"</i>. Dr. Vuran was
  awarded an <b>NSF CAREER award</b> for the project
  ``<a href="http://www.nsf.gov/awardsearch/showAward.do?AwardNumber=0953900" target="no">Bringing Wireless Sensor Networks Underground</a>''. He
  received the <b>2007 ECE Graduate Research Assistant Excellence Award</b> from School of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Georgia Institute of Technology and the <b>2006 Researcher of the Year Award</b> in Broadband and Wireless Networking Laboratory, School of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Georgia Institute of Technology.<br/><br/>
</p></td>
