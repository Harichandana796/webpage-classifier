<p class="MsoNormal"><o:p> </o:p></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span class="GramE"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The
I</span></span><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>-MINDS prototyping
process was initiated in September 2002 using a <st1:place w:st="on"><st1:placename w:st="on">National</st1:placename> <st1:placetype w:st="on">Center</st1:placetype></st1:place>
for Information Technology in Education (NCITE –www.ncite.unl.edu) Seed Grant,
which allowed us to build a prototype software package and conduct preliminary
experiments to evaluate the technical correctness and educational feasibility
of I-MINDS. NCITE is a research center located at the University <span class="GramE">of</span> Nebraska (UNL) and operated jointly by the <st1:place w:st="on"><st1:placetype w:st="on">College</st1:placetype> of <st1:placename w:st="on">Education</st1:placename></st1:place> and Human Sciences, Nebraska
Educational Telecommunications, and the Computer Science and Engineering
Department. The prototype was developed and built in Java.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In May 2003, we
conducted a pilot study.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The system was
used by subjects in a controlled experiment to assess what impact it had on
student learning of Global Information Systems (GIS) content. GIS technology
can be used for scientific investigations, resource management, and development
planning. Tables 2 and 3 document the key specifics of the pilot study. On Day
1, subjects in both groups completed a 109-point pretest of the content that
was to be taught during the two sessions. At the conclusion of the class on Day
1 for both groups, the subset of 60 items that related to the content of that
class was included on the posttest. After the Day 2 instruction, the subset of
49 items that related to the content of that class constituted the posttest.
Subjects in the control group learned the identical content during each of the
two sessions, as did subjects in the experimental group. The difference was
that the control group students were in the same room as the instructor. Their
class was taught in a very traditional manner with the professor using
PowerPoint slides identical to those used for the experimental group to teach
the content. Results for the two testing sessions are encouraging. For Test 2, <i><span style="color:black">the amount that the I-MINDS group improved from the pretest
to the posttest was nearly twice that of the control group</span></i><span style="color:black">. Comments from the university professor who used I-MINDS
in teaching both of the content lessons were also encouraging. He indicated
that the teaching tool was very easy to learn and use. The instructor also
noted that questions asked of him via I-MINDS tended to be higher quality,
reflect a deeper understanding, and demand a richer response than those
questions posed during the control sessions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span><o:p></o:p></span></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:black'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:black'>In
the summer of 2004, we founded a startup company, <a href="http://www.i-minds.com" target="top">I-MINDS, LLC</a>.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:black'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:black'>In
January 2005, we received National Science Foundation (NSF) funding through the
Small Business and Innovation Research (SBIR) program.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:black'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:black'>In
May 2005, we received funding from Microsoft <span class="SpellE">ConferenceXP</span>
Program through the <st1:place w:st="on"><st1:placetype w:st="on">University</st1:placetype>
 of <st1:placename w:st="on">Nebraska</st1:placename></st1:place>.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>From this funding, we have started our <span class="SpellE">ConferenceXP</span>-Powered I-MINDS.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The <span class="SpellE">ConferenceXP</span>
platform provides for us a more stable framework and a set of enabling
applications in terms of archival, networking, and deployment.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:black'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:black'>In <span class="GramE">Spring</span> and Fall 2005, we conducted tests at the Department
of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) of the <st1:place w:st="on"><st1:placetype w:st="on">University</st1:placetype> of <st1:placename w:st="on">Nebraska</st1:placename></st1:place>
and published our results.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span></span><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Specifically, we have added features to
support the Jigsaw procedure, a structured cooperative learning procedure.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We deployed the I-<span class="SpellE">MINDS+Jigsaw</span>
system three lab sessions of CS1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Initial results were encouraging.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>We found out that students, without the benefit of face-to-face
interactions, were able to make use of I-<span class="SpellE">MINDS+Jigsaw</span>,
and performed as well as students with face-to-face interactions in the
post-test of each lab.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>However, we also
observed that students rated their team activities more highly in face-to-face
interactions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Further, we also found
significant correlations between the length of messages sent and a student’s
performance.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>There were also indications
that when a student is critical of his or her peers, this student’s post-test
score would likely be higher.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Also,
there seemed to be hints that group activities helped improve students’
post-test scores.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

Tahoma'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
