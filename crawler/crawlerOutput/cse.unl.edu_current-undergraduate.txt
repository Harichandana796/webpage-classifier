<div class="wdn-inner-wrapper">
<blockquote class="wdn-quote">The one constant is change.

            <cite class="wdn-quoter">Heraclitus</cite>
</blockquote>
<p>A bachelor's in Computer Science is about empowering you with a well-rounded education – a wide knowledge base – so that you can anticipate

            <em>change</em> and embrace the future.

        </p>
<p>The UNL course catalog offers information on courses offered, the course description, and prerequisites.<br/><a class="wdn-button wdn-button-brand" href="http://bulletin.unl.edu/undergraduate/courses/CSCE/">Undergraduate Course Catalog</a></p>
<p>The course schedule lists the current and upcoming course offerings<br/><a class="wdn-button wdn-button-brand" href="courses">Current &amp; Upcoming Course Schedule</a></p>
<p>The four year course offering guide will help you plan your program of study. Students are encouraged to meet with
            their advisers every semester to make sure they are on track towards a degree and that courses taken will be applicable towards graduation.
            <a href="docs/CourseOfferingCycle-2014-03-24.pdf">Download the course offering guide here.</a>
</p>
</div>
</section>

<h3 class="wdn-brand">Majors, Minors, and Focus Areas</h3>
<p> The following provide the degree requirements for graduating with a major in Computer Science or Computer Engineering, a minor in Computer Science, or graduating
            with a Focus – advanced skills in a specific area. UNL does not currently offer a Minor in Computer Engineering or any Engineering discipline. 
             If you matriculated under a different bulletin year, be sure to consult the appropriate bulletin, or schedule a meeting with an Undergraduate adviser. 
        </p>

<h3 class="wdn-brand clear-top">Student Resource Center</h3>
<p>The CSE Student Resource Center located in Avery Hall 12, is staffed by student tutors who are available <span style="white-space: nowrap;">M–F, 9 A.M.–7 P.M.</span>
                    to help you with your CS and CE courses. The staff can also provide technical support on issues such as problems logging in, printing, installing
                    an app, etc. and point you in the right direction if you need additional help. </p>
</div>
<div class="bp2-wdn-col-two-sevenths">
<figure class="wdn-frame clear-top">
<img alt="Gem Mentor" src="ugrad/gem_mentors08-09-web.jpg"/>
</figure>
</div>
</div>
<div class="wen-grid-set">
<div class="bp2-wdn-col-full">
<p>Be sure to utilize your teaching assistants. Teaching assistants for all CS and CE courses hold office hours in the Student Resource Center. Refer
                    to the <a href="https://www.google.com/calendar/embed?src=uib054ip0ecmk8cbggeih70hrg@group.calendar.google.com&amp;ctz=America/Chicago">Online Schedule</a>                    
                    for specific times.</p>
<p>Even if you don’t need help, the Student Resource Center is a great place to study either alone or with your peers.</p>
<p id="src-join-us"><a href="javascript:void(0);">Interested in becoming a Undergraduate Student Resource Tutor?</a>
</p>

<p>We are always looking for great students to work as tutors in the Student Resource Center. If you are interested and meet the following requirements,
                    please email your resume to
                    <a href="mailto:jenna.huttenmaier@unl.edu"> Jenna Huttenmaier.</a>
</p>
<h5 class="uppercase" style="margin-top:10px">Qualifications</h5>
<ul>
<li>Undergraduate, upper-class standing</li>
<li>Computer science or computer engineering major</li>
