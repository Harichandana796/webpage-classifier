<h1>
CSCE310: Data Structures and Algorithms</h1></center>
<p><br/><b>Prereq:         </b>CSCE156
(Intro. to CS II) and CSCE235 (Intro. to Discrete Structures)<b>.</b>
</p><p><b>Course description:</b>  In this course you will learn the basis
of the design and analysis of algorithms.  The following topics will
be addressed, time permitting:   asymptotic analysis, greedy, 
divide and conquer,  search, sort, algorithms on trees, graphs, dynamic
programming, NP-completeness, etc.

</p><p><b>Protocol of the course:</b>
</p><ul>
<li>
Lectures by instructor, 3 times per week.</li>
<li>
The workload will consist of:</li>
<ul>
<li>
<i><u>required reading </u></i>(as indicated in the <b><a href="schedule.htm">Class
schedule</a></b>): the textbook will be followed in a more or less linear
fashion.  It is your responsibility to read the material in the book
and make sure you understand.</li>
<li>
<i><u>programming and theoretical assignments:</u></i>  Programming
assignments <i>must be</i> done in C++ and turned in <img height="12" lowsrc="new.gif" src="new.gif" width="31"/><b>
before 11:59 p.m. on the due date</b>.  Usually, homeworks must be
turned in using the UNIX
<font face="Courier
New,Courier">handin</font>
program on <font face="Courier
New,Courier">cse.unl.edu.</font>  Pen-and-paper
assignments must be given to the instructor<i> </i>right <b>before</b>
the lecture on the due date. Late homeworks are subject to a 20% deduction
per day (including week-ends), any <i>second</i> after the due date counts
as an entire day.   Each student has a 5-day late-return credit,
to be used at discretion. No question asked, no further extensions <i>ever</i>
given. Make sure you inform both instructor and TA when you use your privilege.</li>
<li>
<u><i>tests</i>:</u> There will be a pre-test, a mid-term  and a final. 
Tests cannot be taken in advance.  Tests cannot be made up except
by instructor's permission.</li>
</ul>
<li>
It is the student's responsibility to ensure an account on cse.unl.edu.</li>
<li>
Discussions among students, instructor, and TA are encouraged.  Homeworks
however are a <i>strictly</i> individual activity: <i>no sharing is permitted</i>
(unless when specified by instructor).  Unethical behavior will be
heavily sanctioned (<i>e.g.</i>, a null grade on the task).</li>
<li>
If you decide to work in team (which is not permitted) <i>always</i> acknowledge
any help received from other individuals.</li>
<li>
<i>Always</i> fully reference material used (<i>e.g.</i>, encyclopedia,
book, paper, journal, <b>web site</b>).</li>
<li>
Attendance is not mandatory.  But students are responsible for the
material covered and announcements made during the class.</li>
</ul>
<b>Grading policy:</b>
<ul>
<li>
The plan is to partition the grades as follows:</li>
<ul>
<li>
Pre-test:  5%</li>
<li>
6-8 homework assignments: <img height="12" src="new.gif" width="31"/>35%.
(Programming assignments will be graded as follows: 70% for correctness,
15% for programming style, and 15% for documentation.)</li>
<li>
Midterm: 30%</li>
<li>
Final: 30%</li>
</ul>
<li>
Bonuses will be awarded to students who interact during lecture (answer
questions, solve challenge problems, <i>etc</i>.)</li>
</ul>
