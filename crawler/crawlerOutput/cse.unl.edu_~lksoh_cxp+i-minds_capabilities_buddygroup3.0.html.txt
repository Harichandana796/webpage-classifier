<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom:6.0pt"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#99CC00'>BuddyGroup
3.0<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This BuddyGroup
capability was completed in February 2006 and revised in May 2006, and revised
again in December 2006.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It has advanced
message management, statistics display, and student group support.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>All messages are tracked and themed using a
message tree, allowing I-MINDS to track in fine resolution every group
discussion.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It also computes statistics
on the fly, allowing a student to monitor how his or her buddies (peers) have
been doing in the group.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This capability
is the “bread-and-butter” of I-MINDS: it collects and analyzes data
automatically for our student agents.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>This also provides wonderful opportunities as a testbed for educational
research studies.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>As this is one
of our intelligent capabilities (which are usually significantly complex than
our other capabilities), here we provide a detailed discussion of the features
and requirements, including installation guideline.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><o:p> </o:p></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF6600'>1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Buddy Group<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>1.1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Introduction<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In version 2,
buddy group was its own capability modified from the chat capability included
with ConferenceXP.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In I-MINDS version 3,
buddy group is a capability mode of merged capability.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It retains the same form as the original
capability with text boxes for displaying received messages and entering new
messages.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This mode also retains the
Send button from the original.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Improvements to chat include an additional text box where message you
are responding two is displayed.<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span></b>This text box includes a popup menu with
two items: ‘History’ and ‘Clear’, whose functionality is described in the next<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"> </b>section. This capability also contains
an ‘Options’ menu with three items: ‘Display Statistics’, ‘Database Search’ and
‘Manage Buddy’, the first two of which are described in the next<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"> </b>section.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The ‘Manage Buddy’ option is described, along
with its form, in section 2.4.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In figure
2.1 we show an example of the Buddy Group capability form.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>1.2.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Flow of Control<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Unlike the question answering mode in
merged capability, there is only one version of buddy group.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This mode is designed to be used by both teachers
and students simultaneously.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The buddy
group mode connects to the I-MINDS database through merged capability agent
class, described above.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Participants can
send messages to each other by using the buddy group mode.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These messages are composed into a Message
object using the methods in merged capability agent, described above.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The tags used from table 1.1 are
Buddy_Message and Buddy_Response.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These
Message objects are then multicast using the SendObject method to the rest of
the classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It is important to
mention that only participants running buddy group mode in merged capability
who are in the sender’s classroom will receive the message and only
participants in your buddy group will display it.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This process is described in more detail in section
2.4, below.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In the buddy group mode, it is possible
to select a previously received message by just clicking on it in the message
text box.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The selected message is added
to the response text box.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>While the
response text box contains a message, any new messages sent by the user will be
'in response' to this message. To clear the message just right-click the
response text box to bring up the menu and select 'Clear'.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>To view the Message Tree for the previously
received message just select 'History' from popup menu.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>See figure 2.2 for an example.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This will cause the MessageTree form to
become visible, displaying the tree view for the message tree (figure 2.3).<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>For buddy group we introduce the concept
of Message Trees.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Message Trees provide
the ability for one message to initiate an expanding flow of dialog.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>According to our concept, a Message Tree
starts with a single message--a question asked by one of the students.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This message forms the root of the Message
Tree. <span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> </span>Questions are displayed in bold by
the buddy group form.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When another
student responds to this question it this linkage is stored on the I-MINDS
database. New messages can respond to either the original message or any
previous response.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This leads to a tree-like data structure
and is why refer to them as message trees.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The above linkage is maintained in Message_Tree table in the I-MINDS
database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Because each message has a unique
Message_ID it is possible to construct a Message Tree at any time.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The MessageTree class included in this
project file does just that.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>By using a
recursive algorithm on the I-MINDS database the stored messages can be
extracted and added to a tree view data object to facilitate display.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>1.4.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Buddy Manager<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In the pervious
version of I-MINDS, we provided a dropdown menu of online participants for the
user to select his or her buddies from.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>This had the disadvantages that both the user and her respective buddy
had to be online at the same time, for the buddy relationship to be
established.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In the new version of
I-MINDS we have added an entirely new interface for adding buddies, called the
buddy manager.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   </span>Figure 2.4 shows a
screenshot of the buddy manager form.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The buddy manager becomes visible whenever the user selected ‘Manage
Buddy Form’ from the options menu in the buddy group mode.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The buddy manager form shows all the
participants who are currently stored in the I-MINDS database and indicates
whether they are on or offline.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>There
are two checkboxes for each participant, one for adding the participant as a
buddy and the other for adding her as a buddy, temporarily.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Temp buddies are counted as buddies for
purposes of receiving messages, but the relationship is asymmetric rather than
symmetric.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><!--[if gte vml 1]><v:shape id="_x0000_i1029"
 type="#_x0000_t75" style='width:282pt;height:234.75pt'>
 <v:imagedata src="buddygroup3.0_files/image009.png" o:title=""/>
</v:shape><![endif]--><img border="0" height="313" src="buddygroup3.0_files/image010.jpg" v:shapes="_x0000_i1029" width="376"/><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Figure 2.4:</span></b><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Buddy Manager Form<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Maintaining the
symmetric relationship between buddies requires a fairly complicated
handshaking procedure between the two participants and the I-MINDS
database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When you remove a buddy a
message is created use the Remove_Buddy tag and that message is sent to your
old buddy.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The database immediately
removes your old buddy from your buddy group.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>When your old buddy receives the Remove_Buddy message, her agent will automatically
remove you from her buddy group as well and inform her of this fact.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Adding a new buddy is somewhat more
complicated because we must wait until your new buddy accepts the invitation to
become buddies.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When you check the Buddy
box for a participant, your agent sends an Add_Request message to your
potential buddy.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When the message is
received, her agent will popup a confirmation box asking her if she would like
to add you as a buddy.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Based on her
decision, her agent sends one of the following three response messages:<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Add_Buddy, Response_Not_Added,
Response_Cannot_Add.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The first indicates
that she has added you as a buddy; your agent will then add her to your buddy
group.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The second indicates that she has
declined your offer of being a buddy and the third indicates that she cannot
become your buddy because her buddy group is full.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In version 3, it
is possible to both add and remove buddies who are offline as well as
online.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This is done by maintaining an
offline mailbox of messages in the I-MINDS database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>If you try and add a participant as a buddy
who is offline, your agent will leave her a message in the offline
mailbox.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The next time she logs on her
agent will check its mailbox and present her with the same confirmation
box.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Depending on your status, her agent
will either send a message directly or leave your agent a message with her
decision.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">    </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:14.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>5.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Database<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The I-MINDS
database is currently running MySQL version 5.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>In the future we intend to switch over from MySQL to SQL Server or
something similar.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The basic structure
should easily transfer over to any relational database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The database was designed using a combination
of DBDesigner4, an open source designer from fabForce, and
phpMyAdmin-2.6.3.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For an illustration of
the I-MINDS database consult figure 5.1 below.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The mysqldump textfile of the database can be found in the same folder
as the source code.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Unlike in
previous versions, in version 3 we have completely done away with the
ComponentTestLibarary.dll which contained DataAdapters.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The merged capability class now has its own
database connection object, which is set to look at the DSN described in the
section 6 below.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The merged capability
agent class now contains almost all of the database queries necessary to run
both merged capability and the buddy group and question answering modes.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Certain peripheral classes such as the
MessageTree still maintain their own data tables, but use the database
connections stored in merged capability.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>As you can see

<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF6600'>5.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Change Log<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This section
covers the changes the I-MINDS team has made to the BuddyGroup capability with
version 2.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These changes include
additional features, bug fixes, and database structure changes.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>None of these changes break the functionality
described above.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>5.1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Data Adapters<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>One significant
change we made in this version is how the database is accessed by our source
code.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In v1 most of our source code
other than the statistics class used C# DataAdapters to access the
database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In brief, DataAdapters select
all the records in a table and populate a C# DataTable with the results.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This can be very convenient because once the
DataTable is populated you can manipulate the data (add, remove, update
records) without querying the database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>However, we encountered horrible concurrency issues with this
implementation (see figure 5.1).<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Because
of the way capabilities work, it was very difficult to synchronize all the
DataAdapters.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This led to all sorts of
bizarre errors relating to database concurrency.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The decision was made to remove all the
DataAdapters and replace them with SQL queries throughout the BuddyGroup
codebase.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This eliminated the
concurrency errors because all the students were dealing directly with the
database (see figure 5.2).<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>

<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:24.0pt;text-indent:-24.0pt;mso-list:l1 level2 lfo20;

"Eras Medium ITC";mso-bidi-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">5.2.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'> </span></span></span></b><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>MAX(Session_ID)<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In v1, the
Session ID was determined by simply taking the MAX(Session_ID) from the
database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This worked well until we
started using Question Answering at the same time.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>A scenario occurred where Buddy Group was
started and students joined and queried for their Session ID.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>A Question Answering capability was then
started incrementing the Session ID.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>If
additional students join the Buddy Group they will no longer receive the
correct Session ID.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In v2, the
Session ID is determined once when the capability is created.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It is then broadcast to each participant when
they join the capability.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In affect, the
Session ID is “buffered” for all participants.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Because of this, when the participant who started the capability leaves
the Session ID is not lost.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This also
solves the problem where a student accidentally starts up another Buddy Group
capability.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Now Buddy Group and Question
Answering can coexist peacefully without corrupting the database.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:24.0pt;text-indent:-24.0pt;mso-list:l0 level2 lfo18;
tab-stops:list 24.0pt"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-fareast-font-family:
"Eras Medium ITC";mso-bidi-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">5.3.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'> </span></span></span></b><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Students_In_Buddy_Group
and Students_In_Session<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In v1, both
these tables were N:M linking tables connecting two other tables.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The limitation on N:M linking talbes is that
each pair of values is a unique primary key.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The database will not allow us to have the same student in the same
buddy group twice, for example.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In
affect, we knew which students were in which Buddy Group or Sessions, but that
is all we knew.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We wanted to be able to
track which students where in a buddy group during an interval of sessions and
when students joined and left a session.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>This required more than just N:M linking tables.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Both the above
tables were replaced with triply-linked tables.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Students_In_Buddy_Group now links with the Session table as well as the
previous two.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Students_In_Session now
has a timestamp key for when the student joins the session.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>To reduce the amount of records triply-linked
tables often produce we wanted to store intervals.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This was accomplished by adding a fourth
column to each table which was not a primary key.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When records were added to either table this
fourth column was initially null.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When
the interval was completed this fourth column would be set.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For example when a student is initially added
to Students_In_Buddy_Group a record similar to table 5.1 is added.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When and if the student is removed from the
buddy group this record is updated and the Session_Removed value is set (table
5.2).<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>From this one record we deduce the
student with the identifier <a href="mailto:lmille@cs.eunl.edu">lmille@cs.eunl.edu</a>
was a member of Buddy Group 22 from session 356 to 412.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This saves us 55 records in the database, not
too shabby.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Table 5.2:</span></b><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Students_In_Buddy_Group record<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:24.0pt;text-indent:-24.0pt;mso-list:l0 level2 lfo18;
tab-stops:list 24.0pt"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-fareast-font-family:
"Eras Medium ITC";mso-bidi-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">5.4.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'> </span></span></span></b><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Load
Messages<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In v1, each
message was broadcast to each student immediately after it was added to the
database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This worked fine in practice,
but happens if one of your buddies arrives late?<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We decided it would be nice to have the
option to ask the database for the messages sent to your buddies for this
session.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We added the “Load Messages”
menu item to the Options menu.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Pressing
it queries the database for the messages sent during this session.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This list is filtered by who your buddies
are.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It will not return messages for
students who are not currently your buddies.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>These messages are identically formatted to those received in real time.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>5.5<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>MessageTree<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The MessageTree
class was modified so that it has the ability to generate a full message tree
for any message regardless of its position in the tree.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   </span>This is done by first determining the root
for message tree (the message ID which started the topic) and then generating
the full subtree from this message.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This
reduces the complexity of some of the queries in the Statistics class
considerably.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The MessageTree class can
be configured to only generate the subtree for the given message ID.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:21.0pt;text-indent:-21.0pt;mso-list:l2 level2 lfo19;
tab-stops:list 21.0pt"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-fareast-font-family:
"Eras Medium ITC";mso-bidi-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">5.6<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>  </span></span></span></b><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Statistics<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>It was
determined through testing that several of the statistics queries in v1 where
not returning exactly the results we wanted.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>A minor change to the way we stored messages in the database
considerably reduced the complexity of some of the queries while increasing the
validity of their results.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This was done
by storing whether or not the message was a topic-starter or response in the
Message table.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The Recipient column now
contains the value “BUDDIES-TOPIC” or “BUDDIES-RESPONSE” where
appropriate.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The queries in
v1 for Dialog Involvement only looked at the responses and their parents.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Thus they only worked only for message trees
of max height 2.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In v2, we can query for
all the messages that are “BUDDIES-TOPIC” and use the full message tree feature
described above in MessageTree to compute the statistics for message trees of
any height.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We were also able to clean
up some of the statistics queries in v2 such as those for
ComputeAverageResponseTime.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>All queries
now check the same session interval.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In
v1, ComputeSessionMessages and ComputeSessionMessageToTeacher (along with their
averages) did not use this session interval determined by the Calculate
menu.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This has been fixed in v2.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:21.0pt;text-indent:-21.0pt;mso-list:l2 level2 lfo19;
tab-stops:list 21.0pt"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-fareast-font-family:
"Eras Medium ITC";mso-bidi-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">5.7<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>  </span></span></span></b><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Database
Search<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The Database
Search form can be used by both students and teachers to look up both messages
and users in the database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Messages are
searchable based on the message itself, the sender, the group they were sent
to, by score, by session, and also by time and date the messages were received,
answered, or discarded.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Any combination
of these properties can be used at once thanks to intelligent querying.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Students are searchable by name and
identifier.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The Database Search form is
accessible from the Options menu.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
