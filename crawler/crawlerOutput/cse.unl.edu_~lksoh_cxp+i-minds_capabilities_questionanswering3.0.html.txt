<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom:6.0pt"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#99CC00'>QuestionAnswering
3.0<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><o:p> </o:p></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This QuestionAnswering
capability was completed in December 2005 and revised in May 2006, and revised
again in December 2006.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This capability
has two views: an instructor application and a student application.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The person in charge of the Q&amp;A session
(e.g., an instructor) can pick and choose questions to answer.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The application helps manage the
questions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The users asking questions
(e.g., a student) can review their questions and answers. They can also ask
questions.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This capability
also has agent intelligence.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The
instructor application scores each question based on keywords and also “machine
learns” from the instructor about what keywords are important
behind-the-scenes, without interfering with the instructor’s actions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This allows the capability to learn to score
each question more accurately over time.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>As this is one
of our intelligent capabilities (which are usually significantly complex than
our other capabilities), here we provide a detailed discussion of the features
and requirements, including installation guideline.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><o:p> </o:p></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF6600'>1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Capability<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:.25in;text-indent:-.25in;mso-list:l3 level2 lfo3;
tab-stops:list .25in"><span style='font-size:14.0pt;
font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-fareast-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";
mso-bidi-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-bidi-font-weight:bold'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">1.1.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>        
</span></span></span><span style='font-size:14.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";
mso-bidi-font-weight:bold'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Introduction<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="tab-stops:325.5pt"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-tab-count:1">                                                                                                             </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>As with previous
versions, the question answering mode in version 3 retains both the student and
teacher version.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Both versions were
originally based on the chat capability that comes with ConferenceXP.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The teacher version was heavily modified to
enable lists of previously received questions to be displayed and sorted.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Based on feedback from a test run by <st1:place w:st="on"><st1:placename w:st="on">Bellevue</st1:placename> <st1:placetype w:st="on">University</st1:placetype></st1:place> (www.bellevue.edu) during the
summer of 2006 we have modified the teacher form to make it more user
friendly.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The first major change in the
teacher for is the inclusion of tabs to partition the messages into received,
answered and discarded.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We also added
the ability to reset an answered or discarded message.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The second major change was to allow teachers
to send messages that were not responses.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>This feature is described in more detail below.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Last but not least, teacher can now broadcast
responses to all students, instead of just the one who sent the question.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Both versions have been modified to use the
new message objects rather than the XML tags.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Other than this, the student version remains unchanged in version
3.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Screenshots for the Student and the
Teacher version of Q&amp;A can be found in figure 3.1 and 3.2 respectively.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:.25in;text-indent:-.25in;mso-list:l3 level2 lfo3;
tab-stops:list .25in"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-fareast-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";
mso-bidi-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">1.2.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>        
</span></span></span></b><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Student Flow of Control<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Students using
the question answering mode can ask the teacher a question by entering the
question into the box at the bottom of the Student form (see figure 3.1) and
pressing the Send button.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The question
is then encapsulated using a message object with the QA_Question message type
by the buddy manager agent.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The
resulting message object is then multicast using the SendObject method to the
rest of the classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In affect, the
student version broadcasts the question to all the other instances of the
Question Answering capability (both teacher and student) running on other
machines.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The teacher version will be
listening for such questions.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The student
version also listens for responses sent by the teacher version.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Whenever a message with the QA_Response
message type is received, the sender identifier is checked against the
identifier for the local participant.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>If
a match is found then the response is being sent to this student version.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The actual response content is displayed
along with the original question in the ‘Recent Answer’ and ‘Recent Question’
boxes.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>They are also added to the ‘Prior
Messages’ table.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Figure 3.1 shows how
the student version looks after it has received a response.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Students can resend questions to the teacher
by right-clicking on the question or answer and pressing ‘Resend’ on the popup
menu.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>1.3.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Teacher Flow of Control<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Teachers using
the question answering mode will see the form shown in figure 3.2.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The teacher form displays all the questions
that have been previously asked by students in the classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Questions that have already been answered or
discarded will appear in the appropriate tabs.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Questions that are still waiting for a response will appear in the ‘Received
Questions’ tab, sorted by score.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When
the teacher right-clicks on a message in the ‘Received Questions’ table he or
she has the option of either answering or discarding the question.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>If the teacher selects the question, it will
appear in the ‘Current Question’ box.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The teacher can then write a response to this question and press
‘Answer’ button.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>A new message with the
QA_Response message type is then created with the response and the message
object is then multicast using the SendObject method to the rest of the
classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When the teacher answers or
discards the question, the question answering agent will modify the weights of
the keywords it contains.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>See the next section
for more details. <o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In version 3, we
have added the ability for the teacher to send message to the students rather
than just waiting to respond to their questions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We do this by maintaining a list of students
who are currently in the classroom and running the Q&amp;A mode.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>By selecting individual students or selecting
the ‘To All’ checkbox the teacher can determine which students will receive the
message.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When the teacher presses the
‘Message’ button, whatever she has written is encapsulated into a message
object with the QA_Message message type and sent to all the students who are
selected.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The students can easily
distinguish between messages and responses from the teacher because the Q&amp;A
agent indicates this on the student form.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>As mentioned
above, the teacher version listens for questions asked by students.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When such a question is received the question
answering agent first determines what score the question should receive.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This is done by first parsing and tokenizing
each word in the question.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For more
information please consult the Question Answering Agent section below.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The question immediately appears in the
‘Received Questions’ table and is available to the teacher.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>As this table is sorted by score, good
questions will appear higher on the table while bad questions might not even
appear on the table unless the teacher scrolls down.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>1.4.<span style="mso-tab-count:1">    </span>Intelligent
Agent<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The teacher
version of the question answering mode contains an intelligent agent whose
purpose is to aid the teacher in deciding which questions to answer.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It does this by assigning a score to each
question as it is received from the student.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>This score is determined by the length of the question and the weights
of the keywords it contains.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When a
question is first received it is tokenized and stemmed by a natural language
processing program.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The stems are checked
against the keywords stored in the database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>If a match is found the weight of the keyword is added to a running
total.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This total is then normalized
along with the length of the question to determine the score.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The intelligent
agent also adjusts the keyword weights up or down.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This is done when the teacher decides to
answer or discard a question.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When the
teacher answers a question the question is once against tokenized and
stemmed.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The weights for keywords found
in the question are increased.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Stems
that are not found in the keywords table are added as keywords with a low
weight.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When the teacher discarded a
question the weights for the keywords found in that question are decreased.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The natural
language processing program we are using is called “combiner.exe”.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It maintains a taboo list of certain common
words.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>All keywords are automatically
normalized by the intelligent agent to fit a certain range.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This prevents the weight associated with a
common keyword from getting so large that it dominates the rest. <o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In pervious
versions the machine learning weights were hardcoded for the intelligent
agent.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>There was also only one set of
keyword weights, hardly suitable for a system that should support multiple
teachers and classrooms.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In version 3,
we have added the machine learning weights to the database so they can be
adjusted without recompiling.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Each
classroom has its own set of machine learning weights that the teacher can
modify and the keyword weights are now specific to each teacher and classroom
combination.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This allows the I-MINDS
system to store the same keyword with different weights for two different
classes taught by the same teacher.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>1.5.<span style="mso-tab-count:1">    </span>Adding
Keywords<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The teacher can
at any time select the ‘Add Keyword’ item from the options menu.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This brings up a form containing all the
keywords and weights stored in the database for that teacher and classroom
combination.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Unlike in previous
versions, keywords can no longer be deleted by using this form.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>New keywords can be added by clicking on the
star row at the bottom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Existing
keywords can be updated by selecting their entries and typing in a new
weight.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>To save the changes simply press
the Update button.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>An example of this
form is shown in figure 3.3.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>To further
assist the teacher we have developed the Virtual Classroom and Statistics
Charts modules.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These modules can be
started from the Options menu.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>2.1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Virtual Classroom<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The Virtual
Classroom form is very useful for teachers.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>It displays information about each student, message, and topic from a
classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Students are represented by
small pie charts, with each chart split into five pieces.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Each piece corresponds to a different
statistics category.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The pieces are
colored blue if the student is at or above the thresholds for those statistics
(as set by the teacher) and colored red if the student falls below the set
thresholds.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These thresholds are stored
in the database so the teachers can just “set it and forget it”, rather than
worry about their levels.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The statistics
are computed for the last number of sessions specified at the bottom left
corner of the screen.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>An example of the
Virtual Classroom for can be seen in figure 4.1.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent:.25in"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The messages and
topics for the classroom are displayed in list views on the right hand side of
the screen.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>All information tracked
about each message/topic by I-Minds is displayed in these panes.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Also, just like in Database Search, a message
can be right clicked on, bringing up a “Message Tree” option.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Choosing this option causes a tree describing
the thread containing that message/topic to be drawn for the user to work with.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent:.25in"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Finally, this
form can also be used to find out specific information about individual
students and buddy groups in the classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>A student’s pie chart can be right clicked to display all the messages
by just that one student, or to find out information about their buddy
groups.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>If the “Buddies” option is
chosen, just that student’s buddies are displayed along with their messages and
topics.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Additionally, there are check
boxes for each student which can be checked to get overlapping buddy
groups.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For example, say a teacher
wanted to find out information about all the buddies that both student A and
student B share.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Their check boxes can
be selected and then the teacher can click on the Buddies button at the bottom
of the form to display information about the overlapping buddies.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

</span>Statistics Charts<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The Statistics
Charts form is used to display statistics information about students in
graphical form.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>A teacher can choose to
display all the statistics of any individual student or the ratings of each
student for an individual statistic, both in bar chart form.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Once again, statistics that fall at or above
the thresholds set by teachers are shown in a blue color and those that fall
below thresholds are shown as a red color.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The thresholds are the same ones recorded in the database by the Virtual
Classroom form, and any change to the thresholds is propagated in both
forms.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>There is also the option of
displaying a legend for the statistics by clicking on the Legend button.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Once again, the stats are computed for the
number of sessions specified in the bottom left corner of the screen, which is
also tied to the corresponding option in Virtual Classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For an example of the Statistics Charts form,
consult figure 4.2.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF6600'>3.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Database<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The I-MINDS
database is currently running MySQL version 5.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>In the future we intend to switch over from MySQL to SQL Server or
something similar.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The basic structure
should easily transfer over to any relational database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The database was designed using a combination
of DBDesigner4, an open source designer from fabForce, and phpMyAdmin-2.6.3.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For an illustration of the I-MINDS database
consult figure 5.1 below.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The mysqldump
textfile of the database can be found in the same folder as the source
code.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Unlike in
previous versions, in version 3 we have completely done away with the
ComponentTestLibarary.dll which contained DataAdapters.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The merged capability class now has its own
database connection object, which is set to look at the DSN described in the
section 6 below.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The merged capability
agent class now contains almost all of the database queries necessary to run
both merged capability and the buddy group and question answering modes.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Certain peripheral classes such as the
MessageTree still maintain their own data tables, but use the database
connections stored in merged capability.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>

<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>As you can see
from figure 5.1, the database for I-MINDS version 3 has become fairly
complicated.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It has grown considerably
from the one used in previous versions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>New tables were added to support the concept of classrooms.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>As a matter of fact, the classrooms table is
now the heart of the database replacing the sessions table from version 2.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We have retained the message table but added
several new variables to support future work on agents.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Several new tables have been added to support
the forthcoming structured group mode and the new buddy manager form in the
buddy group mode.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>There are several
satellite tables used for recording statistics on PGM experiments.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The use of these tables will be addressed in
future technical reports.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:14.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>6.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Installation<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>

mysql-connector-odbc-3.51 driver.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We
