<div class="contentTitle">Research Interests</div>
<div class="contentText"><br/> <p>My research is focused at the intersection of
          Artificial Intelligence, Human-Robot Interaction, and Unmanned
          Systems.  I have researched both the ethical ramifications of
          warfare robots and the interactions between humans and robots in
          rescue environments, with a specific focus on small Unmanned
          Aerial Vehicles (see<a href="http://www.smithsonianmag.com/science-nature/why-people-comfortable-with-small-drones-180951433/"> Smithsonian
          Magazine</a>, June 2014).  I approach
          interactions with small Unmanned Aerial Vehicles through an
          exploration of behavior-based robotics and cognitive science
          principles.  Additionally, I have been involved in extensive
          fieldwork at the Center for Robot-Assisted Search and Rescue
          including 6 exercises and a deployment in 2014 to the SR-530
          mudslides in Oso, Washington.</p></div>
<div class="contentTitle">About Me</div>
<div class="contentText"><br/><p>I am an Assistant Professor in the
            Computer Science and Engineering Department at the University of
            Nebraska-Lincoln.  I am a Faculty Member of the <a href="http://nimbus.unl.edu/">NIMBUS Lab</a>, where we study
            unmanned systems (with a focus on aerial robotics).  I completed
            my Ph.D. at Texas A&amp;M University, studying field robotics and
            human-robot interaction through the Center for Robot-Assisted
            Search and Rescue with my advisor Robin Murphy.</p></div>
