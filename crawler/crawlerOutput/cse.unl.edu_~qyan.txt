<p>
      Qiben “Chi-Ben” Yan is an Assistant Professor in the Computer Science and Engineering at University of Nebraska Lincoln. Prior to joining UNL, he worked in a cyber security startup company called Shape Security in the silicon valley, where he participated in building the first “botwall”. He received a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Virginia Tech in 2014, an M.S. and a B.S. degree in Electronic Engineering from Fudan University in Shanghai China in 2010 and 2007, respectively. His primary research interests are in wireless network security and privacy, network monitoring and cyber forensics, botnet and malware detection. He is particular interested in building a data-driven secure network infrastructure - such as anomaly detection system, traffic monitoring and analysis system, attack resilient communication system - to provide security add-ons for the modern networks under cyber threats. He is always interested in problems that have potential real-world impact, and tries to bridge the gap between theory and practice. </p>

<p>
</p><h4>Research areas:</h4>
        Wireless Network Security and Privacy<br/>
<li><em>Wireless physical-layer security; cognitive radio network security; jamming and anti-jamming; device fingerprinting</em></li><br/>
        Network monitoring and forensics<br/>
<li><em>Network diagnosis; root cause analysis; performance diagnosis; intrusion detection</em></li><br/>
        Botnet and malware detection<br/>
<li><em>Botnet detection; mobile malware detection; mobile Apps security analysis</em></li>
<p>
</p><h4>Approach:</h4>
        By digging into the details of communication protocols and systems or analyzing large amount of network data from traffic monitors, I identify real-world vulnerabilities of the current or future networks and their root causes. Then, I design the theories, systems or mechanisms to protect against the potential adversaries who may exploit those vulnerabilities, by applying techniques from other fields, such as machine learning, statistical methods, time series analysis. I emphasize the implementation of my projects, and try to bridge the gap between theory and practice.

<p><strong>Ph.D. RA/TA openings: </strong></p>
<p>We are always looking for self-motivated Ph.D. students to join our group, starting from either Spring or Fall. A successful candidate is expected to carry out research in one of the multiple areas: networking security and privacy, wireless and mobile security, wireless  networking, botnet and malware research.</p>
<p>An ideal candidate is expected to have:</p>
<p>- An earned master degree in EE, CS or a relevant area<br/>
            - Strong mathematical skills, including probability analysis<br/>
            - Solid background in wireless communications and networking<br/>
            - Previous research experience in wireless is a plus<br/>
            - Background and research experience in security or cryptography is a big plus</p>
<p>If you are interested in either position, please send your CV, PS, transcripts, TOEFL and GRE scores, and everything that you believe will help your application to Prof. Qiben Yan in one email. </p>
<p><strong>Visiting scholars:</strong></p>
<p>Visiting/exchange scholars and students who have matching interests and strong research backgrounds are welcome to contact Prof. Qiben Yan for inquiries about position availability. </p><br/>
