<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify;border:none;mso-border-top-alt:
solid windowtext .5pt;mso-border-bottom-alt:solid windowtext .5pt;padding:0in;
mso-padding-alt:1.0pt 0in 1.0pt 0in"><span style="font-size:14.0pt;font-family:
Tahoma;color:blue">Initiative's Objectives<o:p></o:p></span></p>
</div>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><span style="font-size:14.0pt;
font-family:Tahoma;color:#3366FF"><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><span class="SpellE"><span style="font-size:14.0pt;font-family:Tahoma;color:#3366FF">Phenology</span></span><span style="font-size:14.0pt;font-family:Tahoma;color:#3366FF"> studies the seasonal
timing of different developmental stages and the life cycles of plants and
animals.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Understanding the processes
that impact these developmental stages and forecasting <span class="SpellE">phenological</span>
stages are important to growers, food processors, crop insurance and seed
companies,<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>the commodities markets, and
those who enjoy the aesthetics of plants.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Similarly, climate change modeling and satellite-based forecasting
systems need basic <span class="SpellE">phenology</span> networks and models to
interpret their spectral data, especially the “greenness” or photosynthetic
response to the environment.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In Nebraska
and the High Plains region, a <span class="SpellE">phenology</span> network and
the resulting models are needed to:<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>1)
simulate alternative crop adaptation and rotations, 2) better target genotypes
with value added quality traits to the landscape, 3) improve irrigation water
management, 4) design new decision support systems for integrated pest
management and organic <span class="SpellE">agroecosystems</span>, forecast
insect and plant disease outbreaks, and 5) provide the <span class="SpellE">cyberinfrastructure</span>
that can lead to information fusion for complex ecosystems and societies.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><i><span style="font-size:14.0pt;
font-family:Tahoma;color:#3366FF"><o:p> </o:p></span></i></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><i><span style="font-size:14.0pt;
font-family:Tahoma;color:#3366FF"><o:p> </o:p></span></i></p>
<div style="border-top:solid windowtext 1.0pt;border-left:none;border-bottom:
solid windowtext 1.0pt;border-right:none;mso-border-top-alt:solid windowtext .5pt;
mso-border-bottom-alt:solid windowtext .5pt;padding:1.0pt 0in 1.0pt 0in">
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify;border:none;mso-border-top-alt:
solid windowtext .5pt;mso-border-bottom-alt:solid windowtext .5pt;padding:0in;
mso-padding-alt:1.0pt 0in 1.0pt 0in"><span style="font-size:14.0pt;font-family:
Tahoma;color:blue;mso-bidi-font-style:italic">What is <span class="SpellE">Phenology</span>?<o:p></o:p></span></p>
</div>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><span style="font-size:14.0pt;
font-family:Tahoma;color:#3366FF"><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:justify"><span class="SpellE"><span style="font-size:14.0pt;font-family:Tahoma;color:#3366FF">Phenology</span></span><span style="font-size:14.0pt;font-family:Tahoma;color:#3366FF"> is the study of the
seasonal timing of different developmental stages, or more simply the life
cycles of plant and animals (after Bradley et al., 1999).<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><span class="SpellE">Organismal</span> <span class="SpellE">phenological</span> development is driven by thermal time and
modified by genes that control <span class="SpellE">vernalization</span> (or
dormancy) and photoperiod.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>At the
ecosystem level, there is a critical need to understand how the <span class="SpellE">phenology</span> of the myriad of co-existing organisms are
related to each other so that partial information from these organisms can be
used to develop an <span class="SpellE">indepth</span> understanding of<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>the ecosystem.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For example, we hope to integrate information
from key cultivars (wheat, soybeans, corn, grapes, and ornamentals) and
indicator species (prairie grasses or threatened/endangered species) to develop
a rich understanding of ecosystem health and its response to environmental
change.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
