<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom:6.0pt"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#99CC00'>BuddyGroup<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This BuddyGroup
capability was completed in February 2006.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>It has advanced message management, statistics display, and student
group support.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>All messages are tracked
and themed using a message tree, allowing I-MINDS to track in fine resolution
every group discussion.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It also computes
statistics on the fly, allowing a student to monitor how his or her buddies
(peers) have been doing in the group.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This capability
is the “bread-and-butter” of I-MINDS: it collects and analyzes data
automatically for our student agents.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>This also provides wonderful opportunities as a testbed for educational
research studies.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>As this is one
of our intelligent capabilities (which are usually significantly complex than
our other capabilities), here we provide a detailed discussion of the features
and requirements, including installation guideline.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><o:p> </o:p></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF9900'>1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><span style="mso-tab-count:1">   </span>BuddyGroup<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:14.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF9900'>1.1. <span style="mso-tab-count:1"> </span>Introduction<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This capability
was originally modified from the Chat capability included with ConferenceXP.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It has the same text boxes for displaying
received messages and entering new messages.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>This capability also retains the Send button from the original.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Improvements to Chat include an additional text
box where message you are responding two is displayed.<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span></b>This text box includes a
popup menu with two items: ‘History’ and ‘Clear’, whose functionality is
described in the next<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"> </b>section. This
capability also contains an ‘Options’ menu with two items: ‘Display Statistics’
and ‘Manage Buddy’, each described in the next<b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"> </b>section.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>See figure 1.1 for
an example of the Buddy Group capability form.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><!--[if gte vml 1]><v:shape
 id="_x0000_i1025" type="#_x0000_t75" style='width:286.5pt;height:4in'>
 <v:imagedata src="buddygroup_files/image003.png" o:title=""/>
</v:shape><![endif]--><img border="0" height="384" src="buddygroup_files/image004.jpg" v:shapes="_x0000_i1025" width="382"/><o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Figure 1.1. The Buddy Group
capability form<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:
"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF9900'>1.2.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Flow
of Control<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Unlike our QuestionAnswering capability,
there is only one version of BuddyGroup.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>This capability is designed to be used by both teachers and students
simultaneously.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>All instances of
BuddyGroup require a connection to the I-MINDS database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Students can send messages to each other by
using the chat capability.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These
messages are composed into message strings by using the buddy group tags
described below.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These message strings
are then multicast using the SendObject method to the rest of the
classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It is important to mention
that while several participants might be using BuddyGroup simultaneously, only
messages from students who are in your Buddy Group will be displayed.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In the BuddyGroup capability, it is
possible to select a previously received message by just clicking on it in the
message text box.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The selected message
is added to the response text box.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>While
the response text box contains a message, any new messages sent by the user
will be 'in response' to this message. To clear the message just right-click
the response text box to bring up the menu and select 'Clear'.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>To view the Message Tree for the previously
received message just select 'History' from popup menu.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>See figure 1.2 for an example.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This will cause the MessageTree form to
become visible, displaying the tree view for the Message Tree (figure 1.3).<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>

<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center;mso-layout-grid-align:
none;text-autospace:none"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Figure 1.3:<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Message Tree for [16:58] Teacher: some

<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The BuddyGroup capability also tracks a
wide range of statistics based on student message traffic.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These statistics can be viewed by students
for anyone in their buddy group by selecting the ‘Display Statistics’ item in
the ‘Options’ menu.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This will cause the
Statistics form to appear.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For more
information on the statistics tracked consult the documentation in the
Statistics form class.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The ‘Manage Buddies’ item in the Options
menu is actually a drop down list containing all the students in the Venue
(except yourself).<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This list is updated
when students join or leave the Venue.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Students who are already in your buddy group will have checks in front
of their identifiers.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Clicking on a
student who is checked will uncheck the menu item and remove the student from
your buddy group.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Recall, that you will
not receive messages from students unless they are in your buddy group.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Checking a student does the opposite, adding
that student to your buddy group.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:
"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF9900'>1.3.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>New
Concepts<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>It is worth pointing out that messages
for this capability and questions for the QuestionAnswering capability are both
stored in the same table in the I-MINDS database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In fact, from a design point of view we
consider questions and messages to be the same thing.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Said another way, each question is a message
in the I-MINDS database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Questions asked
to the teacher by students (in QuestionAnswering) have their recipient flag set
to 'TEACHER'.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>All messages sent by
BuddyGroup have their recipient flag set to 'BUDDIES'.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In this way, we can guarantee backwards compatibility
with our QuestionAnswering capability.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>For BuddyGroup we introduce the concept
of Message Trees.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Message Trees provide
the ability for one message to initiate an expanding flow of dialog.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>According to our concept, a Message Tree
starts with a single message--a question asked by one of the students.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This message forms the root of the Message
Tree.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Questions are displayed in bold by
the BuddyGroup capability.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When another
student responds to this question it this linkage is stored on the I-MINDS
database. New messages can respond to either the original message or any
previous response.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This leads to a tree-like data structure
and is why refer to them as Message Trees.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The above linkage is maintained in Message_Tree table in the I-MINDS
database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Because each message has a
unique Message_ID it is possible to construct a Message Tree at any time.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The MessageTree class included in this
project file does just that.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>By using a
recursive algorithm on the I-MINDS database the stored messages can be
extracted and added to a tree view data object to facilitate display.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:
"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF9900'>1.4.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Buddy Group Tags<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The teacher message uses the following
format:<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>&lt;to&gt;toBuddies variable&lt;/to&gt;<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>&lt;message&gt;message
string&lt;/message&gt;<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>&lt;ID&gt;message ID&lt;/ID&gt;<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>&lt;from&gt;sender
identifier&lt;/from&gt;<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The Buddy Group capability uses the same
type of message passing architecture as Question Answering.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The only addition is the ID tag.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This tag stores the unique Message_ID from
the database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We felt that this was more
compact and efficient to send both the message and its ID through the
ConferenceXP sendObject method.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In this
way the message content is available to the other instances of the capability
without having to query it from the database but the ID (primary key) is
available when and if it is needed.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This
allows the entire record to be extracted from the database through a simple
select query.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In Buddy Group, the toBuddies variable is
used instead of a specific identifier.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>It is the responsibility of the individual instances of the capability
to filter the messages received.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This is
done by considering the sender identifier.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>This identifier is compared to the list of identifiers in Buddy Group
for this participant.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>If a match is
found the message is displayed, otherwise it is ignored.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">    </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF9900'>2.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Statistics<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:
"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF9900'>2.1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Statistics Tracked<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This class is designed to facilitate the
collection and display of the statistics that are tracked by the Buddy Group
capability.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Currently, there are 14
statistics organized into 5 groups tracked by the Buddy Group capability.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These are summarized below:<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-tab-count:1">            </span><span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Buddy Messages Group<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<ul style="margin-top:0in" type="disc">
<li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l2 level1 lfo6;tab-stops:list .5in;
     mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:
     "Eras Medium ITC"'>The number of messages the student has sent this
     session.<o:p></o:p></span></li>
<li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l2 level1 lfo6;tab-stops:list .5in;
     mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:
     "Eras Medium ITC"'>The average number of messages the student has sent
     over all sessions.<o:p></o:p></span></li>
</ul>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Teacher Messages Group<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<ul style="margin-top:0in" type="disc">
<li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo7;tab-stops:list .5in;
     mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:
     "Eras Medium ITC"'>The number of messages the student has sent to the
     teacher this session.<o:p></o:p></span></li>
<li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l7 level1 lfo7;tab-stops:list .5in;
     mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:
     "Eras Medium ITC"'>The total number of messages the student has sent to
     the teacher.<o:p></o:p></span></li>
</ul>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Responsiveness Group<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<ul style="margin-top:0in" type="disc">
<li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l16 level1 lfo8;tab-stops:list .5in;
     mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:
     "Eras Medium ITC"'>The number of responses sent by each student.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Here response is defined as a message
     with a link to a previous message.<o:p></o:p></span></li>
<li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l16 level1 lfo8;tab-stops:list .5in;
     mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:
     "Eras Medium ITC"'>The average amount of time it takes for the student to
     respond to a question.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This would
     be measured by subtracting the timestamp of the response from that of the

<li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l16 level1 lfo8;tab-stops:list .5in;

<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Dialog Involvement Group<o:p></o:p></span></p>

     they did NOT ask (both count and averages).<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Here involved is defined as the number
     of messages found in the message tree with each question as the root.<o:p></o:p></span></li>

<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>

tab-stops:list .25in;mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:

"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF9900'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">2.2.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>     </span></span></span></b><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:
"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF9900'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Statistics
Menu<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The Statistics form contains two
Menus--'Statistics Visible' and 'Calculate'.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The first menu is nested and contains menu items for all the groups and
all the statistics tracked.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Checking or
unchecking an item causes the column width for that item in the DataGrid to
toggle between visible and invisible.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>There are 'select all' menu items at both the top and the group
level.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Checking 'select all' at the top
level causes all menu items to be selected, while unchecking 'select all' at
the group levels merely unchecks all the items for that group.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The Calculate menu contains four options
to determine how many sessions should be considered by the Statistics queries.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These options are--all, last 5, last 10, last
20 Sessions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Choosing a number of
sessions other than all reduces the amount of time required to compute the
Statistics.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>If there are fewer than X
sessions, choosing last X is equivalent to choosing all sessions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>See figure 2.1 for an example of the Statistics
form.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Figure 2.1:</span></b><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Statistics form for Buddy Group<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF9900'>3.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><span style="mso-tab-count:1">   </span>Database<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The I-MINDS
database is currently running MySQL version 4.1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In the future we intend to switch over from
MySQL to SQL Server or something similar.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The basic structure should easily transfer over to any relational
database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The database was designed
using a combination of DBDesigner4, an open source designer from fabForce, and
phpMyAdmin-2.6.3.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For an illustration of
the I-MINDS database consult figure 3.1 below.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The mysqldump textfile of the database can be found in the same folder
as the source code.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>On the C# side,
the database hook is contained in the ComponentTestLibrary.dll.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This dll, whose source is provided, consists
of a ComponentModel class called DatabaseWrapper and another class called
DatabaseMethods, along with the dataset schema DataSet1.xsd.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The DatabaseWrapper class consists of
numerous DataAdapters designed with the form editors.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The dataset schema was generated from these
DataAdapters using the wizard.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><!--[if gte vml 1]><v:shape id="_x0000_i1028"
 type="#_x0000_t75" style='width:431.25pt;height:285.75pt'>
 <v:imagedata src="buddygroup_files/image011.png" o:title=""/>
</v:shape><![endif]--><img border="0" height="381" src="buddygroup_files/image012.jpg" v:shapes="_x0000_i1028" width="575"/><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Figure 3.1:</span></b><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The DBDesigner model for the I-MINDS database<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Wherever
possible we have tried to conform to the C# database connection model, filling
the dataset from multiple DataAdapters.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The tables in the dataset can then be hooked to DataGrids or DataViews
for display.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We use OdbcCommand builders
to generate both the update and insert queries with two exceptions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We the Keyword_Ranking and Message
DataAdapters we experienced numerous DatabaseConcurrency exceptions which
claimed that 0 rows were updated.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Despite much experimentation we were unable to lock down this
exception.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Our solution was to write our
own Update and Insert queries and run them on each row in the DataTables based
on row states.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This seemed to eliminate
the problem.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF9900'>4.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><span style="mso-tab-count:1">   </span>Installation<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This section
leads you through the installation for ConferenceXP and our capabilities.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The first program to install is the
mysql-connector-odbc-3.51 driver.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We
have included this with our source code.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Next, if you have not already done so download the CXP Client version
3.2.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Before you install it make sure
that the Microsoft Journal Viewer has been installed.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Please consult the ConferenceXP client
installation guide for more information.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>We provide the
database dump file so that users can upload the I-MINDS database to their local
server.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The installation is relatively
straight forward assuming you have access to MySQL server version 4.1 or
higher.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Table 4.1 gives the console
command.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>If your machines do not have
access to the internet you will need to install your own venue service on one
of your machines.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The Venue Service can
be downloaded from the ConferenceXP website. <o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>

iminds.sql<o:p></o:p></span></p>

you could connect to the I-MINDS database running on cse.unl.edu.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The user name and password are given in Table
