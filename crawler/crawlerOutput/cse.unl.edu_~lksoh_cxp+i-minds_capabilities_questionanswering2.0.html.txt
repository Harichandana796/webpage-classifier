<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-bottom:6.0pt"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#99CC00'>QuestionAnswering
2.0<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><o:p> </o:p></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This QuestionAnswering
capability was completed in December 2005 and revised in May 2006.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This capability has two views: an instructor
application and a student application.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The person in charge of the Q&amp;A session (e.g., an instructor) can
pick and choose questions to answer.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The
application helps manage the questions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The users asking questions (e.g., a student) can review their questions
and answers. They can also ask questions.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This capability
also has agent intelligence.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The
instructor application scores each question based on keywords and also “machine
learns” from the instructor about what keywords are important
behind-the-scenes, without interfering with the instructor’s actions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This allows the capability to learn to score
each question more accurately over time.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>As this is one
of our intelligent capabilities (which are usually significantly complex than
our other capabilities), here we provide a detailed discussion of the features
and requirements, including installation guideline.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><o:p> </o:p></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF6600'>1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Capability<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:.25in;text-indent:-.25in;mso-list:l3 level2 lfo3;
tab-stops:list .25in"><span style='font-size:14.0pt;
font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-fareast-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";
mso-bidi-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-bidi-font-weight:bold'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">1.1.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>        
</span></span></span><span style='font-size:14.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";
mso-bidi-font-weight:bold'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Introduction<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The Question and
Answering capability is based on the basic chat capability that comes with
Conference XP.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It uses the communication
protocols common to all capabilities to multicast messages to all participants
in the Venue.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These messages are strings
containing pertinent information such as the identifier who sent the message
bound between xml-style tags which we have defined.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This simplifies the process of parsing the
messages when they are received.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The Question and
Answering capability is designed to run in either the I-MINDS or the CXP
Client.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>There are actually two separate
versions of the capabilities which look the same to CXP—the teacher and student
versions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These versions differ in many
ways, the most significant being their capability forms and the teacher version
connecting to a server-side database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The database is discussed in more detail below although it is worth
pointing out that some tables are updated by the I-MINDS Client.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This means that some tables which should be
updated are not when Q&amp;A is run using the CXP client.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These have minimal impact on the Q&amp;A
client and are summarized in table 1.1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Screenshots for the Student and the Teacher version of Q&amp;A can be
found in figure 1.1 and 1.2 respectively<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:.25in;text-indent:-.25in;mso-list:l3 level2 lfo3;
tab-stops:list .25in"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-fareast-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";
mso-bidi-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">1.2.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'>        
</span></span></span></b><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Student Flow of Control<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Students using
Question Answering can ask the teacher a question by entering the question into
the box at the bottom of the Student form (see figure 1.2) and pressing the
Send button.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The question is then
encapsulated into using the question answering tags described below.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The resulting string is then multicast using
the SendObject method to the rest of the classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In affect, the student version broadcasts the
question to all the other instances of the Question Answering capability (both
teacher and student) running on other machines.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The teacher version will be listening for such questions.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The student
version also listens for responses sent by the teacher version.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Whenever a response is received the
identifier is checked against the identifier for the local participant.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>If a match is found then the response is
being sent to this student version.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The
actual response content is displayed along with the original question in the
‘Recent Answer’ and ‘Recent Question’ boxes.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>They are also added to the ‘Prior Messages’ table.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Figure 1.3 shows how the student version
looks after it has received a response.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Students can resend questions to the teacher by right-clicking on the
question or answer and pressing ‘Resend’ on the popup menu.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>1.3.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Teacher Flow of Control<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Teachers using
the Question Answering capability will see the Teacher form shown in figure
1.1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The Teacher form displays all the
questions that have been previously asked by Students.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Questions that have already been answered or
discarded will appear in the appropriate tables.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Questions that are still waiting for a
response will appear in the ‘Received Questions’ table, sorted by score.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When the teacher right-clicks on a message in
the ‘Received Questions’ table he or she has the option of either answering or
discarding the question.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>If the teacher
chooses to answer the question the sender’s identifier will appear in the send
to label.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The teacher can then write a
response to this question and press send.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The answer is then encapsulated into using the question answering tags
described below.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The resulting string is
then multicast using the SendObject method to the rest of the classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When the teacher answers or discards the
question the question answering agent will modify the weights of the keywords
it contains.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>See the Question Answering
Agent section.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>As mentioned
above, the teacher version listens for questions asked by students.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When such a question is received the question
answering agent first determines what score the question should receive.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This is done by first parsing and tokenizing
each word in the question.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For more
information please consult the Question Answering Agent section below.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The question immediately appears in the
‘Received Questions’ table and is available to the teacher.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>As this table is sorted by score, good
questions will appear higher on the table while bad questions might not even
appear on the table unless the teacher scrolls down.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>1.4.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Question Answering Group Tags<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The student question uses the following
format:<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>&lt;to&gt;toTeacher variable&lt;/to&gt;<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>&lt;question&gt;question
string&lt;/question&gt;<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>&lt;from&gt;sender
identifier&lt;/from&gt;<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">   
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The teacher response uses the following
format:<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>&lt;to&gt; student identifier&lt;/to&gt;<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>&lt;question&gt;question
string&lt;/question&gt;<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>&lt;response&gt;response
string&lt;/response&gt;<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>&lt;from&gt;sender
identifier&lt;/from&gt;<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes">       
</span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="mso-layout-grid-align:none;text-autospace:none"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In Question Answering, the toTeacher
variable is used instead of a specific identifier.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It is the responsibility of the teacher
version of the capability to filter the messages received.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For the teacher version the toTeacher variable
indicates that the string contains a question asked by a student.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The student versions will ignore messages
containing the toTeacher variable and messages containing an identifier that
does not match their local identifier.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:24.0pt;text-indent:-24.0pt;mso-list:l2 level2 lfo4;
tab-stops:list 24.0pt"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-fareast-font-family:
"Eras Medium ITC";mso-bidi-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">1.5.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'> </span></span></span></b><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Intelligent
Agent<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The teacher
version of the question answering capability contains an intelligent agent
whose purpose is to aid the teacher in deciding which questions to answer.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It does this by assigning a score to each
question as it is received from the student.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>This score is determined by the length of the question and the weights
of the keywords it contains.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When a
question is first received it is tokenized and stemmed by a natural language
processing program.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The stems are
checked against the keywords stored in the database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>If a match is found the weight of the keyword
is added to a running total.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This total
is then normalized along with the length of the question to determine the
score.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The intelligent
agent also adjusts the keyword weights up or down.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This is done when the teacher decides to
answer or discard a question.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When the
teacher answers a question the question is once against tokenized and
stemmed.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The weights for keywords found
in the question are increased.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Stems
that are not found in the keywords table are added as keywords with a low
weight.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When the teacher discarded a
question the weights for the keywords found in that question are
decreased.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The natural
language processing program we are using is called “combiner.exe”.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>It maintains a taboo list of certain common
words.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>All keywords are automatically
normalized by the intelligent agent to fit a certain range.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This prevents the weight associated with a
common keyword from getting so large that it dominates the rest. <o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:.5in"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<ul style="margin-top:0in" type="disc">
<ul style="margin-top:0in" type="circle">
<li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l0 level2 lfo1;tab-stops:list 1.0in"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The first of these is that the
      sessionID for messages stored will not accurately reflect the session
      when the message was received.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
      </span>Instead the most current session will be used or 0 if no sessions
      are found. <o:p></o:p></span></li>
<li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l0 level2 lfo1;tab-stops:list 1.0in"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Because the student and teacher
      identifiers are not stored in Q&amp;A it is possible that a sender
      identifier for a message stored will not be found in either table. <o:p></o:p></span></li>
<li class="MsoNormal" style="mso-list:l0 level2 lfo1;tab-stops:list 1.0in"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The StudentsInSession table will
      not be populated at all so no record of which students join the classroom
      (Venue) will be kept.<o:p></o:p></span></li>
</ul>
</ul>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF6600'>2.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Teacher Options<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>To further
assist the teacher we have developed the Virtual Classroom and Statistics
Charts modules.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These modules can be
started from the Options menu.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>2.1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Virtual Classroom<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The Virtual
Classroom form is very useful for teachers.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>It displays information about each student, message, and topic from a
classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Students are represented by
small pie charts, with each chart split into five pieces.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Each piece corresponds to a different
statistics category.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The pieces are
colored blue if the student is at or above the thresholds for those statistics
(as set by the teacher) and colored red if the student falls below the set
thresholds.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These thresholds are stored
in the database so the teachers can just “set it and forget it”, rather than
worry about their levels.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The statistics
are computed for the last number of sessions specified at the bottom left
corner of the screen.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent:.25in"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The messages and
topics for the classroom are displayed in list views on the right hand side of
the screen.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>All information tracked
about each message/topic by I-Minds is displayed in these panes.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Also, just like in Database Search, a message
can be right clicked on, bringing up a “Message Tree” option.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Choosing this option causes a tree describing
the thread containing that message/topic to be drawn for the user to work with.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent:.25in"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Finally, this
form can also be used to find out specific information about individual
students and buddy groups in the classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>A student’s pie chart can be right clicked to display all the messages
by just that one student, or to find out information about their buddy
groups.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>If the “Buddies” option is
chosen, just that student’s buddies are displayed along with their messages and
topics.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Additionally, there are check
boxes for each student which can be checked to get overlapping buddy
groups.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For example, say a teacher
wanted to find out information about all the buddies that both student A and
student B share.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Their check boxes can
be selected and then the teacher can click on the Buddies button at the bottom
of the form to display information about the overlapping buddies.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent:.25in"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>2.3.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Statistics Charts<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The Statistics
Charts form is used to display statistics information about students in
graphical form.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>A teacher can choose to
display all the statistics of any individual student or the ratings of each
student for an individual statistic, both in bar chart form.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Once again, statistics that fall at or above
the thresholds set by teachers are shown in a blue color and those that fall
below thresholds are shown as a red color.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The thresholds are the same ones recorded in the database by the Virtual
Classroom form, and any change to the thresholds is propagated in both
forms.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>There is also the option of
displaying a legend for the statistics by clicking on the Legend button.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Once again, the stats are computed for the
number of sessions specified in the bottom left corner of the screen, which is
also tied to the corresponding option in Virtual Classroom.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="text-indent:.25in"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF6600'>3.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Database<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The I-MINDS
database is currently running MySQL version 4.1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In the future we intend to switch over from
MySQL to SQL Server or something similar.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The basic structure should easily transfer over to any relational
database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The database was designed
using a combination of DBDesigner4, an open source designer from fabForce, and
phpMyAdmin-2.6.3.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>For an illustration of
the I-MINDS database consult figure 3.1 below.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The mysqldump textfile of the database can be found in the same folder
as the source code.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>On the C# side,
the database hook is contained in the ComponentTestLibrary.dll.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This dll, whose source is provided, consists
of a ComponentModel class called DatabaseWrapper and another class called
DatabaseMethods, along with the dataset schema DataSet1.xsd.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The DatabaseWrapper class consists of
numerous DataAdapters designed with the form editors.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The dataset schema was generated from these
DataAdapters using the wizard.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span><o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>

normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Figure 3.1:</span></b><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The DBDesigner model for the I-MINDS database<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p align="center" class="MsoNormal" style="text-align:center"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Wherever
possible we have tried to conform to the C# database connection model, filling
the dataset from multiple DataAdapters.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The tables in the dataset can then be hooked to DataGrids or DataViews
for display.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We use OdbcCommand builders
to generate both the update and insert queries with two exceptions.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>We the Keyword_Ranking and Message DataAdapters
we experienced numerous DatabaseConcurrency exceptions which claimed that 0
rows were updated.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Despite much
experimentation we were unable to lock down this exception.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Our solution was to write our own Update and
Insert queries and run them on each row in the DataTables based on row
states.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This seemed to eliminate the
problem.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-size:16.0pt;font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";color:#FF6600'>4.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Installation<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>

<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>This section
covers the changes the I-MINDS team has made to the BuddyGroup capability with
version 2.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These changes include
additional features, bug fixes, and database structure changes.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>None of these changes break the functionality
described above.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>5.1.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>Data Adapters<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>One significant
change we made in this version is how the database is accessed by our source
code.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In v1 most of our source code in
Question Answering used C# DataAdapters to access the database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In brief, DataAdapters select all the records
in a table and populate a C# DataTable with the results.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This can be very convenient because once the
DataTable is populated you can manipulate the data (add, remove, update
records) without querying the database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>However, we encountered some concurrency problems with the
implementation.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>While these were not as
bad as those encountered by Buddy Group, the decision was made to remove all
the DataAdapters and replace them with SQL queries.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:24.0pt;text-indent:-24.0pt;mso-list:l1 level2 lfo2;
tab-stops:list 24.0pt"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-fareast-font-family:
"Eras Medium ITC";mso-bidi-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">5.2.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'> </span></span></span></b><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Adding
Keywords<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>In v1, there was
no way to add additional keywords to the database without writing the queries
yourself.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This was an oversight on our
part, in the old I-MINDS client we had a form for adding new keywords and
weights.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>When we made the decision to
use the CXP Client, this was a feature that somehow got left out.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>In v2, this feature is back.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The teacher can at any time select the Add
Keyword item from the Options menu.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>This
brings up a form containing a DataGrid with all the keywords and weights stored
in the database.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The user can delete
existing keywords by clicking on the row and pressing delete.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>New keywords can be added by clicking on the
star row at the bottom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Existing
keywords can be updated by selecting their entries and typing in a new
weight.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>To save the changes simply press
the Update button.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>See figure 5.1 for
more information.<o:p></o:p></span></p>

Classroom<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The Virtual
Classroom form is very useful for teachers.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>It displays information about each student, message, and topic from a
classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Students are represented by
small pie charts, with each chart split into five pieces.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Each piece corresponds to a different
statistics category.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The pieces are
colored blue if the student is at or above the thresholds for those statistics
(as set by the teacher) and colored red if the student falls below the set thresholds.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>These thresholds are stored in the database
so the teachers can just “set it and forget it”, rather than worry about their
levels.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The statistics are computed for
the last number of sessions specified at the bottom left corner of the screen.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The Virtual Classroom form is accessible from
the Options menu, in the teacher version.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal" style="margin-left:24.0pt;text-indent:-24.0pt;mso-list:l1 level2 lfo2;
tab-stops:list 24.0pt"><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:
normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC";mso-fareast-font-family:
"Eras Medium ITC";mso-bidi-font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><span style="mso-list:Ignore">5.4.<span style='font:7.0pt "Times New Roman"'> </span></span></span></b><b style="mso-bidi-font-weight:normal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>Statistics
Charts<o:p></o:p></span></b></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'><o:p> </o:p></span></p>
<p class="MsoNormal"><span style='font-family:"Eras Medium ITC"'>The Statistics
Charts form is used to display statistics information about students in
graphical form.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>A teacher can choose to
display all the statistics of any individual student or the ratings of each
student for an individual statistic, both in bar chart form.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Once again, statistics that fall at or above
the thresholds set by teachers are shown in a blue color and those that fall
below thresholds are shown as a red color.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes"> 
</span>The thresholds are the same ones recorded in the database by the Virtual
Classroom form, and any change to the thresholds is propagated in both
forms.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>There is also the option of
displaying a legend for the statistics by clicking on the Legend button.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>Once again, the stats are computed for the
number of sessions specified in the bottom left corner of the screen, which is
also tied to the corresponding option in Virtual Classroom.<span style="mso-spacerun:yes">  </span>The Student Charts form is accessible from
the Options menu, in the teacher version.<o:p></o:p></span></p>
